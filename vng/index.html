03

Compliance Positioning

This is the money section. Address their core question head-on with the two-layer solution.

Quinn frames the problem

  • "Vietnam's PDPL requires personal data to be collected, processed, and stored in Vietnam. At the same time, Decree 147 and Directive 57 require age verification. This is exactly why Roblox pulled their age verification feature here."
  • "We've solved this exact challenge in Singapore, Indonesia, and Australia — k-ID has a proven playbook."

BJ explains the solution

Layer 1 — On-Device Facial Age Estimation

Image captured, analyzed, and deleted entirely on the user's phone. Biometric data never leaves the device — never leaves Vietnam. k-ID receives only the age result + anti-spoofing confirmation.

Fast, frictionless, no PII transfer. Live and working in other markets today.

Layer 2 — Local Verification Provider

User submits government ID (VNeID/CCCD) to a licensed Vietnamese eKYC provider. Provider processes entirely on Vietnamese servers, deletes the image. k-ID receives only the age signal — no name, address, ID number, or biometrics.

Same model as SingPass (Singapore), IKD (Indonesia), banking apps (Australia). Integration timeline: weeks, not months.

The line
  • "We've architected our solution specifically so that personal data stays in the country it belongs in. That's how we operate globally, and it's how we'll operate in Vietnam."
Do NOT say
  • "We're compliant" — legal confirmation still pending
  • "Roblox uses our verification product" — they use our knowledge base / regulatory database, not the product
